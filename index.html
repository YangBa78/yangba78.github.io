About

I am a final-year Ph.D. student in Data Science, Analytics and Engineering at Arizona State University. I previously earned my M.S. in Business Analytics from the University of Connecticut, co-supervised by Rong Pan and Michelle V. Mancenido, and worked with the DHS Center for Accelerating Operational Efficiency (DHS-CAOE).

My research interests span machine learning and generative AI, with a focus on large language models (LLMs), multimodal learning, and synthetic data generation. I study how foundation models learn, represent, and generalize across modalities, exploring synthetic data benefits/risks, LLM calibration, and multimodal latent space alignment. I also have a strong background in statistical modeling and data analysis.
