About Publications

Yang Ba

I am a final-year Ph.D. student in Data Science, Analytics and Engineering at Arizona State University, School of Computing and Augmented Intelligence (ASU SCAI), where I am co-supervised by Dr. Rong Pan and Dr. Michelle V. Mancenido, and worked with the DHS Center for Accelerating Operational Efficiency (DHS-CAOE). I previously earned my M.S. in Business Analytics from the University of Connecticut.

My research interests include machine learning and generative AI, focusing on large language models (LLMs), multimodal learning, and synthetic data quality and divesity. I study how foundation models learn, represent, and generalize across modalities, exploring synthetic data benefits and risks, LLM calibration, and multimodal latent space alignment. I also have a strong background in statistical modeling and data analytics.
